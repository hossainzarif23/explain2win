// 01 · TL;DR

What is this, in one read?

Explain2Win is a study tool that helps you learn by explaining topics out loud. You pick a topic (or come from the knowledge graph), record yourself teaching it, and the app does the rest.

The audio is uploaded to S3, transcribed by Google Gemini, then evaluated on five dimensions (correctness, clarity, depth, relevance, structure). The result becomes a permanent Explanation attached to a Study Session. You can re-record to improve — the system compares attempts and tracks your mastery streak.

Next, the AI generates a personalized quiz from your transcription, scored and stored. Over time, a force-directed knowledge graph of every topic you’ve explored visualizes your learning map, with Stripe-powered subscriptions unlocking all four AI student personas and unlimited usage.

// 02 · Concept

The pedagogical idea

A modern interpretation of the Feynman Technique, made measurable.

🎯

Pick a topic

Start from scratch, continue a study session, or jump off from an AI-suggested node in your knowledge graph.

🗣

Teach it out loud

Record up to 10 minutes (Opus / WebM, 16 kHz mono). Speaking forces articulation — the gap between “thinking you know it” and actually knowing it.

🧠

Get evaluated

Five-dimension rubric scored 0–10, with strengths, improvements, missing concepts, and learning objectives extracted.

❓

Take a quiz

Multiple-choice + short-answer questions generated from your actual words. The questions adapt to your chosen student persona.

📈

Iterate

Re-explain the same topic, compare your attempts side-by-side, and watch your mastery streak grow.

🕸

See your map

Every topic becomes a node in a personal knowledge graph, with mastery colour-coded and AI-suggested neighbours.

// 05 · Features

What users actually do

1
Sign in
Google or GitHub OAuth, or email/password (bcrypt). On first login, a User, UserProgress, UserPreferences, Subscription (Free tier), and Credits (50/mo) row are created.
2
Explore the graph
A 2D force-directed canvas (react-force-graph-2d) shows every topic you’ve studied. New topics get a “suggested” flag. Click a node to start a session.
3
Record an explanation
On /explain: choose a student persona, set the scope statement, hit record. A live waveform visualizer shows audio levels. Min 10 s, max 10 min.
4
Receive evaluation
Within ~20 s, the EvaluationCard shows an overall score (e.g. 8.9 / 10), per-dimension bars, strengths, improvements, missing concepts, and learning objectives.
5
Take the quiz
3–10 questions, mix of MCQ and short answer, 60 s per question. The grader compares your answer to the model answer with a forgiving similarity check.
6
Review & iterate
/explanations/[id] shows full transcript + audio player + feedback. /study-sessions/[id] compares multiple attempts with a radar chart and score-delta badge.
7
Watch mastery grow
/progress shows streak, totals, time-series charts (recharts). /question-bank stores all generated questions for spaced repetition.
8
Upgrade (optional)
/billing shows three tiers. Stripe checkout creates a Subscription, the webhook refills credits, and a tier upgrade unlocks all four student personas.

// 08 · Monetization

Tiers & credit costs

Stripe-driven subscriptions. Free tier is fully usable; paid tiers unlock more student personas, more credits, and longer audio retention.

Free
Default for every new user
  • ✓ 50 credits / month
  • ✓ 7-day audio retention
  • ✓ 50 question bank slots
  • ✓ 1 student persona (Curious)
Most popular
Pro
For active learners
  • ✓ 500 credits / month
  • ✓ 30-day audio retention
  • ✓ Unlimited question bank
  • ✓ All 4 student personas
Premium
Power users & teachers
  • ✓ Unlimited credits
  • ✓ Permanent audio retention
  • ✓ Unlimited question bank
  • ✓ All personas + priority
